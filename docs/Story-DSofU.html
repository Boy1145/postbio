The Dark Side of the University

Part I: The Computer Leading to the Other Side

In essence, a university is a crucible of emotion. No matter how polished and alluring its public persona may appear on the surface, it inevitably harbors a darker, more clandestine side deep within. Among these hidden facets, none is more prominent than the myriad emotions exuded by the students—ranging from positive sentiments such as joy and pride to negative ones like disappointment and despair. Legend has it that an ancient computer—rumored to have been tucked away in the library’s basement since the university’s very inception—serves as the gateway to the very core where these negative emotions converge: the so-called "Dark Side of the University."

To this day, the computer sits gathering dust, a relic of an exceptionally antiquated model. No one remembers how to boot it up anymore. Although countless individuals have attempted to power it on, their efforts have met with failure almost without exception. Yet, a select few did manage to stumble upon the secret method of gaining access; of that handful, however, only one ultimately survived. When this sole survivor returned, their mind had shattered; they babbled incoherently, ceaselessly muttering fragmented phrases such as "a wall made of monitors," "a wall made of mirrors," "I saw myself," "disappointment," and "fear." These vague, nonsensical ramblings provided neither concrete evidence nor any new leads. Thus, these scattered fragments constitute the second—and final—clue regarding the enduring mystery of whether the "Dark Side of the University" truly exists.

Part II:The Cabinet that is filled with Other Things

Much like that old computer, this object appears to be a relic dating back to the university's very inception. It is a mysterious filing cabinet—also coated in a layer of dust—and despite being constructed of metal, it bears the unmistakable patina of age. No one knows how to open it; there is no visible lock, nor any sign of forced entry or tampering, yet it simply refuses to yield. The labels affixed to it date back twenty years, yet to this day, no one knows what lies within.

University investigators once brought the mentally unstable survivor back to this location in the hope of unearthing new clues. However, the survivor merely stood there in a daze—except when he approached the cabinet. Whenever he drew near it, his entire body would begin to convulse, as if an invisible wall stood in his path, barring him from getting any closer.

Rumors circulate that this cabinet is filled with the negative thoughts and actions of every student who has passed through these halls—the despair of receiving poor grades, the fear associated with cheating on exams, and so on. Every misdeed committed and every negative emotion experienced by every student is said to be recorded within it in meticulous detail. Of course... who can really say?

Part III:The Resentment-Laden Cylinder Model

Much like the other two long-standing, massive artifacts on campus, this model is coated in a thick layer of dust and draped in cobwebs. It appears to be constructed from cardboard; the tallest cylinder stands nearly twice my own height, and the collection varies wildly in size—some cylinders are exceptionally wide, while others are quite slender. This model has existed for precisely as long as the university itself. However, unlike the other artifacts, this particular model has actually been subjected to an investigation—marking the first of the only two concrete leads we have regarding these mysteries.

After various university personnel exhausted every conceivable method of inquiry to no avail, the administration was compelled to summon a renowned exorcist to assess the situation. The exorcist detected a powerful, intense energy emanating specifically from this model. He offered us numerous insights into its nature: he claimed the cylinders were physical manifestations of the students' accumulated resentment. He explained that the height of each cylinder corresponded to the intensity of student resentment during a specific era, while its width indicated the sheer number of students harboring such negative emotions. He even claimed to hear a cacophony of anguished cries—the collective grievances of students past and present: "Why are there classes at 7 and 8 in the morning? I can't get out of bed!" "Did the professors conspire against us? All the exams are scheduled for the same day—four exams in a single day!"—and a myriad of other such complaints. Yet, when he attempted to delve deeper into his investigation, he recoiled as if he had glimpsed some monstrous entity, and fled the scene immediately. Since that day, nothing has ever been heard from that exorcist again. Consequently, these "clues" remain unverified; nevertheless, they mark the very genesis of the legends surrounding the "Dark Side of the University." Who, after all, can truly discern whether these tales are based on genuine evidence or are merely the product of rumors passed from person to person?
